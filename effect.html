Effect

On biodiversity

High temperatures and severe weather disturbances can damage ecosystems and alter the conditions and cycles of plant reproduction. Resource scarcity and climate change are changing animal habits and migration cycles. We have witnessed the disappearance of many species, including endemic species, or conversely, invasions of invasive species that threaten crops and other animals.

Therefore, global warming affects biodiversity. The balance of biodiversity is changing and threatened. According to the IPCC, an average increase of 1.5 ° C (2.7 ° F) may result in 20-30% species extinction. If the Earth warms above 2°C, most ecosystems will be in trouble.

On oceans

Frozen soil and ice melted in large quantities at both poles, increasing sea level at an unprecedented rate. For a century, the increase has reached 18 cm (including 6 cm in the last 20 years). In the worst case, by 2100, the maximum growth was 1 million.

On humans

Sea level rise and floods cause population migration. The small island nation is at the forefront. By 2050, the estimated number of climate refugees is 250 million.

On the weather

Meteorologists and climatologists around the world have been paying attention to the effects of global warming on weather phenomena. The impact is huge: more droughts and heat waves, more precipitation, more natural disasters such as floods, hurricanes, storms and wildfires, frost-free seasons, etc.
